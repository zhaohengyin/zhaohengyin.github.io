◤ Biography

I am a first-year Computer Science Ph.D. student at UC Berkeley. I work on machine learning and robotics at the Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Lab (BAIR). I am advised by Prof. Pieter Abbeel.

Previously, I obtained my M.Phil. degree in Electronic and Computer Engineering at HKUST in 2023, advised by Prof. Qifeng Chen. Before that, I obtained B.Sc. degree in Computer Science and Mathematics advised by Prof. Wu-Jun Li at Nanjing University in 2021. During M.Phil. research I was also fortunate to work with Prof. Yang Gao and Prof. Xiaolong Wang.

◤ Research

I am interested in understanding how an intelligent, embodied agent can acquire, store, and use knowledge of its surrounding world. Before my Ph.D. journey, I worked on reinforcement learning and imitation learning. I proposed imitation learning algorithms that can leverage cross domain expert data, learn more sample efficiently with MCTS planning, and generalize better with relational inductive bias. I also studied the application of robot learning algorithms on real robotics problem such as dexterous manipulation.
